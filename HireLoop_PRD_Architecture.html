Product requirements & system architecture

HireLoop

An AI-assisted recruitment pipeline, from brief to hire.

This document defines what HireLoop does and how it's built — covering the full pipeline from product brief through JD generation, resume screening, interview question generation, note capture, and candidate scoring.

OwnerProduct / Talent Acquisition Tech
StatusDraft for review
Primary usersRecruiters, hiring managers, interview panel

01Document overview

HireLoop replaces a chain of disconnected manual steps — spreadsheets, email threads, ad hoc interview notes — with one pipeline where each stage feeds structured data into the next, and every AI-assisted decision has a human checkpoint before it affects a candidate.

02Problem & goals

Recruiters currently handle JD writing, resume screening, question prep, and scoring as disconnected manual steps. This causes inconsistent JDs, slow triage on high-volume roles, interviews that vary by interviewer, and subjective, hard-to-defend hiring decisions.

Goals

  • Cut time-to-shortlist by automating resume screening against the JD
  • Standardize interview quality with JD- and resume-aware question generation
  • Make hiring decisions defensible with structured, rubric-based scoring
  • Keep a full audit trail of JD versions, screening scores, and interview scores

Out of scope for v1

  • Fully autonomous rejection of candidates without recruiter approval
  • Offer-letter generation and background verification
  • Native video interviewing — integrate with existing tools instead

04Functional requirements — the pipeline

Each stage below maps directly to a system module in the architecture section. Stages marked checkpoint require explicit human approval before the pipeline continues.

1
Product / brand brief Structured form — company, product, team, seniority, must-haves, budget, location. Saveable as a reusable template.
2
JD generation (AI) Draft summary, responsibilities, and skills from the brief. Recruiter edits inline; full version history retained.
3
Resume intake Bulk ZIP/folder upload — PDF, DOCX, scanned images with OCR fallback. Auto-parsed into a structured candidate profile.
4
Screening & ranking checkpoint Semantic match between resume and JD, with a visible score breakdown. Recruiter must approve the shortlist before anyone is marked not-a-fit.
5
Interview question generation Per-candidate mix of technical, resume-specific, and behavioral questions, each tagged with what it tests.
6
Interview & notes capture Structured notes, one block per question, per candidate. Optional transcription and auto-summarization.
7
AI scoring checkpoint Notes scored against a weighted rubric. Per-criterion scorecard, never a single opaque number — recruiter can override with a note.
8
Decision dashboard Side-by-side candidate comparison — resume score, interview score, composite score. Exportable for the hiring committee.

Human-in-the-loop checkpoints

  1. JD is drafted by AI, but must be reviewed and published by a recruiter
  2. Shortlist ranking is AI-generated, but rejection requires recruiter confirmation
  3. Generated questions can be edited or removed by the panel before use
  4. AI scores are shown with per-criterion reasoning and can be overridden with a note
Compliance note — the pipeline never auto-rejects a candidate. Every reject decision needs a recruiter's explicit action, and it's logged in the audit trail.

Security & compliance

  • Encrypt candidate PII at rest and in transit; restrict resume access by role
  • Retain full version history for JDs, scores, and rubric changes
  • Mask name/gender-coded fields before AI screening, to reduce bias risk
  • Log AI-generated decisions separately from human overrides, for bias audits
